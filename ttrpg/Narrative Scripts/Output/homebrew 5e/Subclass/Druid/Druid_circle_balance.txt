Step 3: Brainstorm New Features
Level 2 Feature: A unique ability that defines the subclass's theme. It should offer a different approach to nature, distinct from existing circles. It has an impact in combat. There is one defining feature and a few minor ones to complete the level.
Level 6 Feature: An enhancement or expansion of the level 2 ability or a new ability that complements the subclass theme.
Level 10 Feature: A powerful ability that solidifies the subclass's role in a party, ensuring it remains balanced with other classes and subclasses.
Level 14 Feature: A capstone ability that embodies the full realization of the subclass theme.
Each level might have more than one feature, but only one is defining.

Step 4: Balancing Considerations
When creating new features that enhance existing ones, make sure they are consistent with each other.
Ensure Balance with Existing Abilities: New features should neither overshadow nor duplicate the core abilities of the Druid class or existing subclasses.
Maintain Thematic Consistency: The features should align with the overarching theme of the subclass, ensuring a cohesive play experience.
Consider Game Mechanics: The subclass should offer unique mechanical benefits, avoiding overpowered combinations or underwhelming abilities.
Step 5: Creative Space for Innovation
Introduce Novel Concepts: Explore uncharted aspects of nature or magic within the Druid class's scope.
Foster Synergy: Design features that synergize with the Druid's core abilities and spellcasting.
With these steps, we can craft a Druid subclass that is balanced, distinct, and thematic, enriching the diversity of options available to Druid players.
