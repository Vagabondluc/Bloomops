1. **Drinking Contest**: 
   - **Setup**: Two or more characters compete in drinking pints of ale.
   - **Mechanics**: Each round, players make a Constitution saving throw, with the DC increasing after each round. 
   - **Variations**: Allow players to use Intimidation to psych out opponents or double the drinks for a round to increase the opponent's DC. Consider adding rewards or using the contest as a plot device for gathering information.

2. **Dinosaur Racing**:
   - **Setup**: Characters race on dinosaurs, such as ankylosaurs, triceratops, or a T-rex.
   - **Mechanics**: Each turn, riders make Animal Handling checks against the dinosaur's DC to determine speed. Lashing the dino can increase speed but risks slowing it down later.
   - **Variations**: Introduce an 'unchained' version where dinosaurs can attack each other.

3. **Gambling Games**:
   - **Simple Version**: Players roll a d20, with the highest roll winning.
   - **Complex Version (Mercer's Gambit of Ord)**: Players roll a d8, d6, and d4 in sequence, betting, calling, or folding between rolls. The total is revealed at the end, with the highest number winning.
   - **Usage**: This can be a chance for players to win rewards or introduce new plot elements.

4. **Shell Games**:
   - **Setup**: A charlatan shuffles bowls or shells hiding an object, often in a busy street setting.
   - **Mechanics**: Players make Intelligence or Perception checks to track the correct cup. The shuffler makes a Sleight of Hand check to move the object secretly.
   - **Variations**: Introduce magical elements or special abilities to increase the challenge.

5. **Maze Challenge**:
   - **Setup**: A maze with traps and shifting corridors.
   - **Mechanics**: Players make Intelligence checks to memorize routes and navigate the maze. Creativity, like leaving trails, can reduce difficulty.
   - **Usage**: This can be part of a larger quest or a standalone challenge.

6. **Rat Races**:
   - **Setup**: Small critters race down a track.
   - **Mechanics**: Players can bet on critters or train their own. 
   - **Usage**: Introduce this as a festival event or casual betting game in a town.

7. **Cooking Competition**:
   - **Setup**: Characters participate in a cooking contest with fantasy ingredients.
   - **Mechanics**: Players make checks using cook's utensils, with additional checks for Perception (taste) or Intelligence (timing). Experimenting with ingredients involves a d100 roll.
   - **Usage**: This can be a fun diversion or part of a larger festival or event.

8. **Archery Contests**:
   - **Setup**: A competition involving targets at varying distances and conditions.
   - **Mechanics**: Players make ranged attack rolls against different ACs. Introduce moving targets or obstacles for complexity.
   - **Variations**: Include rivals or special conditions like limited visibility.

9. **Dragonchess**:
   - **Setup**: A chess-like game with unique pieces and boards.
   - **Mechanics**: Players make opposed skill checks, with the first to three successes winning.
   - **Variations**: Allow Insight and Deception checks to influence the game's outcome.

10. **Fight Clubs**:
    - **Setup**: Bare-knuckle brawling or more dangerous combat.
    - **Mechanics**: Treated like a combat encounter but focused on unarmed strikes. Party members can subtly influence the fight.
    - **Usage**: Include betting to increase stakes, and vary opponents' capabilities.

11. **Strength Contests**:
    - **Setup**: Events inspired by traditional strength challenges like the caber toss.
    - **Mechanics**: Primarily Athletics checks, potentially across multiple rounds or against multiple competitors.
    - **Usage**: Use in festivals or as a test of strength in storylines.

12. **Trebuchet**:
    - **Setup**: A game where characters throw sandbags backward over their head into a basket.
    - **Mechanics**: Players make Dexterity checks with disadvantage to land the sandbag in the target.
    - **Usage**: Suitable for festivals or casual competition among characters.

13. **Corn Maze Game**:
    - **Setup**: A maze in a harvest festival setting.
    - **Mechanics**: Skill challenges involving Athletics, Survival, and Intelligence checks. Players solve puzzles and overcome magical obstacles.
    - **Usage**: Can be part of a festival or a quest to retrieve an item from the maze's center.

14. **Billiards**:
    - **Setup**: A tavern game of skill.
    - **Mechanics**: Players make checks (Dexterity, Sleight of Hand, or Performance) to sink balls. The difficulty increases with each success.
    - **Variations**: Include trick shots for added challenge and strategy.

15. **Beer Pong**:
    - **Setup**: A drinking game where players throw balls into cups.
    - **Mechanics**: Players roll d20s for Dexterity checks, increasing the difficulty each round. Drinking affects a character's sobriety score, determined by a d8 roll plus CON modifier.
    - **Usage**: Good for tavern scenes or casual party interactions.

16. **Darts**:
    - **Setup**: A classic tavern game.
    - **Mechanics**: Players roll two d20s for scoring, with the option to modify rolls with their ranged attack modifier. Scoring varies based on the dice rolls.
    - **Usage**: Can be a quick diversion in a tavern or part of a larger event.
